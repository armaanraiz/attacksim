Getting Started

1 Create Your Account

Sign up for AttackSim with your email address. You'll need to verify your email to get started.

2 Give Consent for Training

Before participating in simulations, you must explicitly consent to receive training emails and simulations. You can revoke this consent at any time from your dashboard.

3 Complete Your First Simulation

Start with a basic phishing email simulation. Take your time to analyze the content and look for warning signs before making your decision.

4 Track Your Progress

Monitor your improvement through the dashboard. Review your detection rates and learn from the educational content provided after each simulation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my personal data safe?

Yes, absolutely. AttackSim is designed for educational purposes only. We don't collect any real sensitive data during simulations. All interactions are logged for learning purposes only, and you can opt out at any time.

Can I opt out of training simulations?

Yes, you can revoke your consent at any time from your dashboard. Once you opt out, you won't receive any further simulations, but you can still access educational content and your historical progress.

What should I do if I receive a suspicious email outside of training?

If you receive a suspicious email in your real inbox: 1) Don't click any links or download attachments, 2) Report it to your IT security team, 3) Delete the email. Use the skills you've learned in AttackSim to identify red flags like urgent language, suspicious senders, and unusual requests.

How often will I receive training simulations?

The frequency depends on your organization's settings and your learning progress. Typically, you might receive 1-2 simulations per week, with more frequency for users who need additional practice.

What happens if I fall for a simulation?

Don't worry! The whole point is to learn from mistakes in a safe environment. You'll immediately see educational content explaining what to look for next time. There are no penalties - only learning opportunities.

Safety & Ethics

Our Commitments

  • Explicit consent required for all training
  • No real data collection during simulations
  • Immediate educational content after each simulation
  • Right to opt out at any time

Best Practices

  • Take time to analyze each simulation carefully
  • Review educational content even if you succeed
  • Apply learned skills to real-world situations
  • Share knowledge with colleagues (when appropriate)
